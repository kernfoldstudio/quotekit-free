How to write a mobile detailing estimate.

A useful estimate tells the customer what is included, what could change, and what the work is expected to cost. Use this checklist before sending the quote.

The minimum useful estimate

Include these six parts.

1. Vehicle and condition

Record the vehicle size and the condition you observed. Note anything that changes the time, materials, or risk.

2. Service scope

Name the selected package and list the work included. Avoid relying on a package name alone.

3. Add-ons and adjustments

Show pet hair, stain treatment, travel, oversize-vehicle work, or other adjustments as separate lines.

4. Total and tax treatment

State the estimated total and whether applicable taxes are included or will be added.

5. Timing, validity, and exclusions

State how long the estimate remains valid and what is excluded or requires inspection.

6. Customer approval

Ask the customer to approve the scope and estimate before work begins. Keep the approved version with your job records.

Common estimate problems

Avoid ambiguity before the appointment.

  • A price with no scope: customers cannot tell what the package covers.
  • Unlisted condition work: heavy soil, pet hair, stains, or excessive cargo can change the job.
  • No change rule: state that a material change in scope may change the final price.
  • No validity window: prices, availability, and the vehicle condition can change over time.
  • No written approval: confirm the estimate and scope before starting.
